I have had a rather unconventional career path. I studied Anthropology at UC Riverside because I was interested in people, culture, and history. My first job in tech combined my interest in those fields with my love of technology — conducting user research at [Luvocracy](http://www.luvocracy.com/). I next worked in support at [Eventbrite](https://www.eventbrite.com/) and [Peek](https://www.peek.com/pro/), although in reality, I wore a lot of hats. From BizOps to Sales to Finance to Content to Support, I worked on every team you can think of. 

But it wasn't until a few years ago that I really started to try my hand at coding. What started as casual interest bloomed into an obsession, and I was grateful to transition to the engineering & product team at [Peek](https://www.peek.com/pro/) where I developed front-end features for Peek.com and managed product development for the Peek Pro Growth team. In 2017 I left Peek to go on an extended honeymoon. Since then, my husband and I have been to 20+ countries, learning new things along the way — like Spanish, leatherworking, scuba diving, and full-stack programming.

Today, I am teaching myself how to be a better programmer, web developer, and product owner one project at a time.
