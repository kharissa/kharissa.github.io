I have had a rather unconventional career path. I studied Anthropology at UC Riverside because I was interested in people, culture, and history. My first jobs in tech combined my interest in those fields with my love of technology — conducting user research at [Luvocracy](http://www.luvocracy.com/) and developing best practices in attendee support at [Eventbrite](https://www.eventbrite.com/). At [Peek](https://www.peek.com/pro/), I wore a lot of hats. From planning and facilitating off-site company retreats for 100+ people to problem solving highly technical questions from our customers and partners — I worked on every team and project you can think of. 

It is thanks to these diverse opportunities at Peek that I was able to develop my interest in more technical roles, which bloomed into an opportunity to transition to the engineering & product team where I developed front-end features for Peek.com and managed product development for the Peek Pro Growth team. In 2017 I left Peek to travel. Since then, my husband and I have been to 20+ countries, learning new things along the way — like Spanish, leatherworking, scuba diving, and full-stack programming.

Today, I am teaching myself how to be a better programmer, web developer, and product owner one project at a time.
